How to Read a Paper

1.INTRODUCTION

Learning to efficiently read a paper is a critical but rarely taught skill. Beginning graduate students, therefore, must learn on their own using trial and error. Students waste much effort in the process and are frequently driven to frustration.

2.The Three-Pass Approach

The key idea:read the paper in up to three passes, instead of starting at the beginning to the end. First pass: give you a general idea about the paper. Second pass: grasp the paper's content but not the details. Third pass: helps to understand the paper in depth

2.1 FIRST PASS

Quick scan to get a bird's-eye view of the paper.

  1. Carefully read the title, abstract and introduction.
  2. Read the section and sub-section headings, but ignore everthing else.
  3. Glance at the mathematicals theorticals foundations.
  4. Read the conclusions.
  5. Glance over the references, mentally ticking off the ones you've read.

At the end of the first pass, answer the five C

  1. Category: What type of the paper is this?
  2. Content: Which other paper is it related to? Which theoretical bases were used?
  3. Correctness: Do the assumptions appear to be valid?
  4. Contributions: What the paper's main contributions?
  5. Clarity: Is the paper well written?

2.2 SECOND PASS

Read the paper with greater care, but ignore details such as proof. It helps to jot down the key points, or make comments in the margins.

  1. Look carefully at the figures, diagrams and other illustarions in the paper. Pay attention to graphs. Are the axes properly labeled? Are the results shown with error bars.

  2. Remember to mark revlent unread references for further reading. The second pass should take up to at least an hour. Then choose to : (a) set the paper aside. (b) return to paper later. (c) go on the third pass.

2.3 THIRD PASS

The key to the third pass is to attempt to virtually re-implemented the paper. During the pass, you should also jot down the ideads for the future works.
